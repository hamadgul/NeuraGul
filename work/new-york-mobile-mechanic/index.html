CASE STUDY — 002 / WEB · CLIENT SITE · 2026

New York Mobile Mechanic

A breakdown at 2am doesn't wait for business hours. Neither does this site — built to rank, load fast, and turn panic into a phone call.

PLATFORM
Web · Next.js
SCOPE
Design → Build → SEO
STACK
TypeScript · Tailwind · Framer Motion
YEAR
2026
— 01 / THE PROBLEM

On-demand repair is won in the search results.

When your car dies on the BQE, you call whoever ranks — nobody comparison-shops from the shoulder. The business needed more than a brochure: it needed to own "mobile mechanic near me" across the five boroughs, on a page fast enough to load on roadside LTE.

— 02 / THE APPROACH

Fully custom. No templates, no page builders.

A ground-up Next.js build engineered around local intent — service and borough pages, structured data, green Core Web Vitals — wrapped in a high-energy visual layer where every scroll funnels to one action: the call button.

FEATURE 01 — LOCAL SEO · NEXT.JS

Built
to rank.

A landing-page matrix of service × borough, schema markup on every route, and Core Web Vitals in the green — the technical groundwork that turns searches into first-position calls.

FEATURE 02 — FRAMER MOTION

Numbers
that move.

Animated stat gauges count up as they enter view — jobs completed, average response minutes, star rating. Energy that builds trust without cluttering the pitch.

FEATURE 03 — CONVERSION

Panic →
phone call.

Live Google reviews for instant credibility and a one-tap call-to-book that follows the scroll. The whole site is a funnel with exactly one exit: the phone.
